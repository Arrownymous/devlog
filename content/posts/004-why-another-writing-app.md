I started writing as a hobby and realized I wanted a fun, simple way to share short stories with other people. At the same time, I wanted to learn some new technologies, so I figured, why not build my own platform?
That idea eventually became Inkwell.

## What I'm building

Inkwell is a place where people can:

Post short stories, micro‑fiction, creepypastas, or experimental drafts.
Follow writers they enjoy and discover new voices.
Build and organize the fictional worlds they’re creating.
Write immediately without dealing with heavy structure or complex setup.

It’s meant to feel lightweight and inviting. You open it, you write, you share.

## The fundamental problem

The Purpose (Not Really a “Problem”)
I’m not trying to solve some major industry issue.
I just noticed something simple: not every writer wants to publish a 300‑page novel. Many of us just like writing quick stories or creative bursts. Existing platforms can feel overwhelming or too focused on long‑form work, so I wanted a space that encourages small, spontaneous creativity.

## What Inkwell is trying to be

Fast. Immediate. Opinionated. You open it, you write. The structure emerges from the work, not from an architecture you set up in advance. Also I'm trying to create a social platform where people can structure there complex worlds they create.

This first entry is mostly me documenting why I started. The next ones will get into the actual build — what I'm choosing, why, and what breaks.

Let's go.
